How a cover gets made

Four steps.
One fit.

11

Step 1 — at your place or ours

Measure

Width, depth, drop, every curve and cut-out — taken down to the millimetre, on site or in the shop. Photos help; the tape decides.

Step 2 — chalk on cloth

Pattern

Measurements become a pattern, chalked straight onto the fabric. Seam allowances, zip lines and fold points marked before a single cut.

Step 3 — the machine room

Stitch

Double-needle seams, reinforced corners, and the closure the piece demands — zips, elastic, ties, velcro or eyelets.

Step 4 — on the piece itself

Fit

The cover goes on the actual piece. If it doesn’t sit flush, it goes back on the table. It leaves when it fits — not before.

Fabric, chosen for the job

Five workhorses
on the shelf.

Canvas

Tough, breathable, takes dust and daily handling. The default for indoor furniture.

Rexine

Leather look, wipe-clean surface. Good for seating that sees spills.

PVC-coated

Waterproof. The monsoon answer for terraces, machines and anything near a window.

Quilted

Padded protection for pieces that travel or get stacked — instruments, glass tops.

Mesh

Air through, dust out. For electronics and appliances that need to breathe.
